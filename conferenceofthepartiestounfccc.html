Conference of the Parties to UNFCCC

Agenda - Realizing the promise of Paris Agreement.

Across the world, countries have realized the dire necessity to mitigate the effects of carbon emission leading to disruptive weather patterns and a noticeable warmer temperature affecting the various biomes, with an urgency to bring about resolutions binding in nature for the countries to act and attenuate the effects of the GreenHouse Gases (GHGs). There is consensus built around a more sustainable approach to development with an onus on the developed countries to wade through and offer assistance to develop infrastructure and technological support to the developing countries. While the Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) required by each country that binds the nations to declare their plans and policies for cutting down the emissions, the reluctance of ratifying the Paris Agreement for a few countries stems from the point of view of heavy economic dependence on the sectors that have the highest emission, eg: Iran’s 77 % of total emission is from the energy sector. Economic sanctions also play an important role in Iran’s failure to curb its in house emission and developing a robust renewable, less polluting energy sector. The Enhanced Transparency Framework (ETF) constructed for the countries to report their practices and policies has yet to bear results as most countries have come up with targets on paper yet implementation of such is still lacking. Many of the G20 nations are failing miserably to cut emissions and lacking the urgency to come up with sustainable policies and willingness required in the current times to attain the goal of keeping global warming below 2 degrees and pursuing it to be limited to 1.5 degrees. With such lackadaisical approach towards a grave issue which threatens the sustenance of all living beings and the environmental integrity, it’s prudent for this committee to discuss and implement tangible solutions before we reach the point of ‘No Return’.
